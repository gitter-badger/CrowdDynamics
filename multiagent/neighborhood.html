5.9. Neighborhoods¶

Neighborhood of agent \(i\) is denoted \(N_i^{neigh} \subset N \setminus \{i\}\) is a subset of agents whose distance from agent \(i\) is smaller than \(d \geq 0\)

\[N_i^{neigh} = \{j \mid j \in N, j \neq i,  \quad \operatorname{dist}(i, j) \leq d  \}\]

Distance function can be defined in different ways depending on the use. For example, distance from the center of masses

\[\begin{split}\operatorname{dist}(i, j) &= \| x_i - x_j \| \\ &= \| \tilde{x} \|\end{split}\]

or skin-to-skin distance of agents

\[\operatorname{dist}(i, j) = h_{ij}.\]

We can also limit the size of the neighborhood \(|N_{neigh}|\) to contain certain number of the closest agents.

Maximum limit of the neighbourhood size is a packing problem. We use following equation to estimate the limit

\[| N_{neigh} | \leq \rho \frac{A_{neigh}}{A_{agent}}\]

where \(\rho\) is packing density coefficient denoting of the maximum percentage of area that can be filled by the agents.

Neighborhood can used for speeding up iterating over agents for updating social forces, for egress congestion algorithms or herding algorithms.

Number of iterations over all agents with naive algorithm has computational complexity of

\[\frac{(|N| - 1)^2}{2} \in \mathcal{O}(|N|^2)\]

Using neighborhood we have

\[|N| | N_{neigh} | \in \mathcal{O}(|N|)\]

When \(| N | > | N_{neigh} |\) we can obtain speed up. We have to keep in mind that naive updation of the neighborhood is still \(\mathcal{O}(|N|^2)\). Updation of the neighbourhood can be splitted to be partially updated every interval to avoid large jumps in computational time for smoother visualization for example.

5.9.2. Updating neighborhood¶

Neighborhood needs to be updated at some time interval \(t \geq \Delta t\) that is larger than the integration timestep. Higher value reduces computational cost but increases error.

Maximum agent velocity \(v_{max}\)

  • Maximum error of social force \(\varepsilon\)
  • Higher speeds require larger neighborhoods (at least in the direction of the velocity).

5.9.3. Circular neighborhood¶

For circular agents in circular neighborhood

\[|N_{neigh}| \leq \rho \left(\frac{R}{r}\right)^2\]

where

  • \(R\) is the radius of the neighborhood
  • \(r\) is the mean radius of the agents
  • Estimates for \(\rho \in [0.7, 0.8]\) can be obtained from circle packing in a circle problem.

Social force error without scaling parameter \(k\) is the distance of the magnitude from zero

\[\begin{split}\varepsilon &= \| \mathbf{f}^{soc} / k \| - 0 \\ &= \left(\frac{1}{a \tau^{2}}\right) \left(\frac{2}{\tau} + \frac{1}{\tau_{0}}\right) \exp\left (-\frac{\tau}{\tau_{0}}\right ) \left\| \left(\tilde{\mathbf{v}} -\frac{a \tilde{\mathbf{x}} + b \tilde{\mathbf{v}}}{d} \right) \right\| \\\end{split}\]

Maximum error \(\max(\varepsilon)\) is found when two agents are in head to head collision, mathematically

\[\varphi = \angle(\tilde{\mathbf{x}}, \tilde{\mathbf{v}}) = \pi\]

Head on collision minimizes time-to-collision

\[\tau = \tau_{min} = \frac{\| \tilde{\mathbf{x}} \| - \tilde{r}}{\| \tilde{\mathbf{v}} \|}\]

Vector part of the eqation

\[\max\left(\left\| \left(\tilde{\mathbf{v}} -\frac{a \tilde{\mathbf{x}} + b \tilde{\mathbf{v}}}{d} \right) \right\| \right) = \| \tilde{\mathbf{v}} \|\]

Error

\[\varepsilon = \frac{1}{\| \tilde{\mathbf{v}} \|} \left(\frac{1}{\tau_{min}^{2}}\right) \left(\frac{2}{\tau_{min}} + \frac{1}{\tau_{0}}\right) \exp\left (-\frac{\tau_{min}}{\tau_{0}}\right )\]

By subsituting

\[\begin{split}\| \tilde{\mathbf{x}} \| &= R - 2 t v_{max} \\ \| \tilde{\mathbf{v}} \| &= 2 v_{max} \\ \tilde{r} &= 2 r\end{split}\]
\[\tau_{min} = \frac{R/2 - r}{v_{max}} - t\]
\[\begin{split}r &\in [0.21, 0.27] \\ v_{max} &\leq 1.5 \\ t &\geq \Delta t\end{split}\]
\[\varepsilon(R, v_{max}, t) = \frac{1}{2 v_{max}} \left(\frac{1}{\tau_{min}^{2}}\right) \left(\frac{2}{\tau_{min}} + \frac{1}{\tau_{0}}\right) \exp\left (-\frac{\tau_{min}}{\tau_{0}}\right )\]
