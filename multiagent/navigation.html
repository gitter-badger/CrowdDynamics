4.5. Navigation¶

  • \(\Omega\) Domain
  • \(\mathcal{A}\) Area occupied by agent
  • \(\mathcal{O}\) Area occupied by obstacle
  • \(\mathcal{E}\) Area occupied by exit
  • \(\Omega \setminus (\mathcal{O} \cup \mathcal{A})\) Unoccupied area

4.5.1. Distance map and static potential¶

Navigation aka path planning is related to continuos shortest path problem, which can se solved by solving Eikonal equation,

\[\left \| \nabla S(\mathbf{x}) \right \| = \frac{1}{f(\mathbf{x})}, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \Omega\]

where \(S(\mathbf{x})\) is distance map, which denotes the shortest time to travel from \(\mathbf{x}\) to destination, which is given by boundary conditions. Function \(f(\mathbf{x})\) is the speed at \(\mathbf{x}\) defined

\[f : \bar{\Omega} \mapsto (0, +\infty)\]

Boundary conditions of the distance map define the value at the the destination

\[S(\mathbf{x}) = 0, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{E}\]

and inside obstacles

\[S(\mathbf{x}) \to \infty, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{O}\]

We get static potential by defining speed in walkable areas \(\Omega \setminus \mathcal{O}\) and inside obstacles \(\mathcal{O}\)

\[\begin{split}f(\mathbf{x}) &= 1, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \Omega \setminus \mathcal{O} \\ f(\mathbf{x}) &\to 0, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{O}\end{split}\]

Target direction

\[\hat{\mathbf{e}}_{S} = -\frac{\nabla S(\mathbf{x})}{\| \nabla S(\mathbf{x}) \|}\]

4.5.2. Dynamic potential¶

Dynamics potential takes into account the positions of the agents in the field. Equation

\[\left \| \nabla T(\mathbf{x}) \right \| = \frac{1}{f(\mathbf{x})}, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \Omega\]

and boundary conditions

\[\begin{split}f(\mathbf{x}) &= 1, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \Omega \setminus (\mathcal{O} \cup \mathcal{A}) \\ f(\mathbf{x}) &\leq 1, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{A} \\ f(\mathbf{x}) &\to 0, \quad \mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{O}\end{split}\]
\[\frac{1}{f(\mathbf{x})} = 1 + \max \left( 0, c_{0} \left( 1 + c_{1} \frac{\mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla S(\mathbf{x})}{v_{0} \| \nabla S(\mathbf{x}) \|} \right) \right)\]
  • \(c_{0}\) general impact strength
  • \(c_{1}\) impact of the moving direction of an agent

Target direction

\[\hat{\mathbf{e}}_{T} = -\frac{\nabla T(\mathbf{x})}{\| \nabla T(\mathbf{x}) \|}\]
